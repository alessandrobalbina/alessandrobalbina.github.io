Other Projects and Passions

This page is dedicated to non-academic experiences and initiatives.

AGESCI

I have been an active member of AGESCI (Italian Catholic Guides and Scouts Association) since 2007, when I joined as a ten-year-old scout. My journey began with the Cagliari 5 group, located in the neighborhood where I was born and raised, and to which I still belong today.

In 2018, I transitioned into leadership. After completing the rigorous three-stage basic training course, in 2025 I was awarded the Wood Badge, a globally recognized symbol of leadership completion in the scouting movement. My training is always in progress, and I am committed to improving as a leader for young generations.

My commitment to AGESCI has grown to include regional and local leadership responsibilities. Since October 2025, I have served as the group leader for Cagliari 5, as well as the Chief Communication Officer for AGESCI Sardegna. Looking ahead, I am excited to participate in the upcoming 26th World Scout Jamboree in Gdańsk, Poland, in 2027.

Passions & Personal Projects

Since childhood, I have been passionate about hiking and outdoor activities in general, as well as photography, which keeps me company on every trail. I have explored numerous corners of Sardinia, with a strong preference for mountain trails—the gorge of Pischina Urtadala and the Sa Giuntura waterfall are undoubtedly my favorite spots. Beyond my home region, I have hiked through the trails of Val Codera, the Brenta Dolomites, and the Circeo National Park.

In my free time, I enjoy diving into vibe coding, exploring programming in a creative and dynamic way. Currently, I am drafting the concept for a digital tool that allows local businesses to network and manage a collaborative online "natural shopping center." I also own a 3D printer and try to design and print things of my own—though sometimes with questionable results.
